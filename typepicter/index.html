Ryan Holmberg wrote an article on the Typepicter a couple of days ago. Ascii art has always fascinated me, but I have since childhood - when playing with my fathers typewriter - been annoyed that there weren't any graphic units. Luckily Uwasa Masato invented a complex typewriter, called a Typepicter, in the early twentieth century that had semantic as well as non-semantic units. Based on the few images supplied I created a small font that resembles the Typepicter. As of now it only has a few units and not the 118 of the original. But it is enough to create a cherry tree. The font and svgs are available at github.
